Where I've been

I was born & raised in Seattle, WA. In 2008, I earned my Bachelor of Science in Nursing but quickly realized being a nurse wasn't my passion. As a result, I joined the family business and worked as an insurance agent while I figured out what I really wanted to do for a career. After lots of self-reflection and research, I decided to pursue a career that better suited my personality and interests. I had started teaching myself how to code via online tutorials, and had also taken a class at a community college, before officially deciding to take the plunge, quit my job, and enroll in a full-time immersive course in full-stack web development. I haven't looked back since!

Where I am now

I am extremely fortunate to be a web developer working for a fantastic company full of hard-working and collaborative people in Austin, TX. I currently work primarily with a CMS as well as HTML, CSS, jQuery, JavaScript, C#, and ASP.NET.

Every day, I learn something new - and I love that! Not only am I learning on the job, but I also enjoy listening to podcasts in my car or on my runs in order to gain insights, advice, and inspiration from other developers.

Outside of work, you can find me running around the lake in downtown Austin with my dog or hiking the mountains when I'm back in Seattle. I recently ran my first half-marathon which was an incredible experience. I also enjoy watching & playing sports, traveling, and of course, spending time with family and friends.

Where I'd like to go

I would love to compliment my coding skills by learning more about UX design. I've always had a great interest in human-centered design. The psychology behind why and how certain details can impact a user's perceptions about or interactions with a website fascinates me. I look forward to continuing to build and improve sites to give users the best experience possible and to help boost business performance, but I also wouldn't mind learning more about the WHY behind what I build.

My ultimate goal is to continue to learn and improve my coding skills so I can grow into the potential I know I have as developer - not just for myself, but also for my team members and employer. Learning is a life-long journey!
