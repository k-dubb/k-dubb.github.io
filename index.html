Where I've been

I was born and raised in Seattle, WA then went to school in Helena, MT where I earned my Bachelor of Science in Nursing. However, I quickly realized nursing wasn't my passion. While trying to figure out what I really wanted to do for a career, I worked as an insurance agent where I was able to gain valuable sales and customer service experience.

After lots of self-reflection and research, I decided to pursue a career that better suited my personality and interests. I quit my job and took a full-stack web development immersive course. I absolutely loved it! I was living in Austin, TX at this time and got a full-time position as a web developer soon after the class ended. I was able to continue learning new coding languages and frameworks on the job, which was fantastic!

Where I am now

I recently got an incredible opportunity to participate in Microsoft's LEAP program as a Program Manager. I moved back to Washington and for the last several months, I've been learning a lot while leading multiple projects as an apprentice. I'm so grateful to have had the chance to try out different roles within the tech industry. Every day, I learn something new - and I love that!

Outside of work, you can find me hiking with my dog or spending time with family and friends. I also enjoy watching & playing sports and traveling. Earlier this year, I ran my first half-marathon which was an incredible and life-changing experience.

Where I'd like to go

Although I enjoy programming, I'm aboslutely loving the Program Manager role! I'd love to compliment this position by learning more about human-centered design. I've always had a fascination with the psychology behind why and how certain details can impact a user's perceptions about or interactions with a product.

My ultimate goal is to continue to gain experience and talk to as many people as I can so I can learn and grow into the potential I know I have as Program Manager - not just for myself, but also for my team members and employer. Learning is a life-long journey!
